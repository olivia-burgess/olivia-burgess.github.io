Emotional Intelligence: A Journey to Self-Improvement

17/6/2024

by Olivia Burgess

What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional intelligence, or EQ, is the ability to understand and manage my own emotions, as well as the emotions of others. It involves being aware of my feelings, being able to regulate them, and using this awareness to manage my interactions with others effectively. EQ is crucial for building strong relationships, making informed decisions, and navigating social complexities with empathy and understanding.

What is the Difference Between EQ and IQ?

EQ and IQ are often compared but serve different purposes. IQ, or intelligence quotient, measures cognitive abilities such as logical reasoning, problem-solving skills, and analytical thinking. In contrast, EQ focuses on emotional and social skills, including empathy, self-awareness, and interpersonal communication. While IQ is important for academic and professional success, EQ is equally essential for personal growth, emotional well-being, and the ability to collaborate and connect with others.

How I Will Be Increasing My EQ and Neuroplasticity

To enhance my EQ, I plan to practice mindfulness and self-reflection regularly. This involves paying attention to my emotional responses and understanding their triggers. Additionally, I will engage in active listening (listening and looping) to improve my interactions with others. To boost neuroplasticity, I will challenge my brain with new activities and learning experiences, such as codewars and codedex.
